We are a group of young enthusiasts who came together to solve the problem of global warming and deforestation to preserve the life on earth.

We collaborate with colleges and universitites to encourage and motivate students to contribute towards mankind by helping grow trees and enable sustainable development.

We have been successful in our motive and since we established in 2018, over a million trees have been grown and thousands of acres of barren land has been restored.

FAQ's (Frequently Asked Questions)

  1. How do you plant a seedling or sapling?

    2. There are actually two major methods of tree planting. One is planting a tree with an intact root ball. The trees can either be bound by fabric and string or potted in a plastic container.

  2. When is the season for planting trees?

    3. "Bare-root" tree planting is done during dormant winter months, most often after December 15th but before March 31st.

  3. Do I really need to mulch my new tree?

    4. New seedlings and saplings need plenty of moisture. Lack of water is the main cause of severe stress to newly planted trees. Mulch is a trees best friend.

  4. Where can I buy trees to plant?

    5. Trees can be purchased in most states at private, industry and government nurseries.

  5. Where should you plant a seedling or sapling?

    6. Use common sense when planting a tree. If the tree is expected to grow tall or expand widely give it the room it needs for future growth. Understanding species moisture, light and soil needs is extremely important.

  6. How many trees are planted in the United States?

    7. Hundreds of nurseries in the United States grow over 1.5 billion trees annually, which reforests nearly three million acres. This number represents over six trees.
